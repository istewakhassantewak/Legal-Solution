Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about consultations, fees, timelines, and how we work with new and returning clients.

1. What should I bring to my first consultation?
Bring any documents related to your matter—contracts, court papers, correspondence, and a short timeline of events. A list of questions helps us focus on what matters most to you in the first meeting.
2. How are legal fees structured?
Depending on the case, we may offer flat fees, hourly billing, or blended arrangements. We explain estimates, billing cycles, and likely expenses up front so you can make informed decisions.
3. How quickly can you start on my case?
After conflict checks and an engagement letter, we can often begin within a few business days. Urgent deadlines—filings, hearings, or negotiations—are prioritized when you tell us the dates involved.
4. Do you offer a free initial consultation?
We offer a limited free consultation for qualifying matters so you can understand options and next steps. If your issue needs a deeper review, we will recommend the best path and fee arrangement before work begins.
